# Ads

## The principle — the 3-second hallway test

Imagine a tech scrolling past your ad on her phone while waiting between scans. You have three seconds. If she can't read the *number* or the *direct claim* in three seconds, the ad fails.

## Static sizes

| Surface              | Pixels       | Aspect |
| -------------------- | ------------ | ------ |
| Instagram feed       | 1080×1350    | 4:5    |
| Instagram story      | 1080×1920    | 9:16   |
| LinkedIn feed        | 1200×1200    | 1:1    |
| LinkedIn sponsored   | 1200×627     | 1.91:1 |
| Google display       | 300×250 / 728×90 / 160×600 | various |
| Newsletter sponsorship | 600×300    | 2:1    |

## Composition — four elements only

1. **Headline.** One line, ≤ 9 words. `display-lg` or `h1` Geist. Sentence case.
2. **Subhead.** One line, ≤ 14 words. `body-md` ink/700.
3. **CTA.** One pill button, `brand/600` bg, white text, ≤ 3 words.
4. **Mark.** Lockup, bottom-right or bottom-center, sized so it's legible but not loud.

That's it. No fifth element. No decorative graphic competing with the headline.

## Backgrounds — pick one per ad

- **`paper`** — most common. Type and number do the work.
- **`brand/900`** — closer / urgency ads. White type, white pill CTA with `ink/900` text.
- **`brand/50` wash** — subtle but on-brand for awareness ads.
- **Single real photograph** — only if it's a real tech in a real environment with a 60% ink/900 scrim under the type. Never stock.

Never mix backgrounds in a campaign — the campaign's identity is *its color*.

## What's never in an ad

- Two CTAs.
- Three headlines.
- Stock smiling people.
- "Tap to learn more 👉" sticker overlays.
- Any emoji.
- Comparison vs. a named competitor by name. (Imply the category — "stop using spreadsheets" — but don't name a vendor.)
- "Discount" / "Limited time" / countdown timers. We don't run that brand.

## Example scripts

### Instagram feed (4:5)
- Bg: paper.
- Headline (top, left-aligned, 56px Geist, ink/900, 4 lines max):
  `Stop tracking your`
  `license in a`
  `spreadsheet.`
- Subhead (below, 20px Inter, ink/700, max 2 lines):
  `One screen for ARRT, IEMA, and modality-specific CE.`
- Pill CTA bottom-left, 56px tall: `Try X-Ray Tech free`
- Lockup bottom-right, 140px wide.

### LinkedIn 1.91:1
- Bg: brand/900.
- Left half: `mono-xl` "287" in white, 220px.
- Right half: `h1` Geist white "Days until your team's next renewal — and you'd know if there were fewer."
- Pill CTA "See the manager view" white bg, ink/900 text.
- Lockup-inverse bottom-left.

### Newsletter banner (600×300)
- Bg: paper.
- Single line headline, 36px Geist, ink/900: `Your license, by the number.`
- Single line subhead, 16px Inter, ink/500: `X-Ray Tech is credential tracking for radiologic technologists.`
- Inline pill CTA right: `Start free`
- Lockup bottom-right, 90px wide.

## Short-form video (15-second ads)

If we produce video — which is rare — the format is:

```
00:00 – 03:00   Hold a still cover frame. Headline + lockup. (Three seconds to read.)
03:00 – 10:00   Show the dashboard. Number counter animates from 0 → 24.
10:00 – 14:00   Close with CTA frame. brand/900 background, white pill CTA.
14:00 – 15:00   Logo hold.
```

- **No voiceover** unless the script is good enough to be quoted in print.
- **No music** beyond an instrumental ambient track. No drops. No hype.
- **Captions on**, baked in. Inter 24px white with 60% ink/900 scrim.

## Paid copy voice

Cut **40% tighter** than homepage voice. If a homepage headline is 8 words, an ad headline is 5.

| Homepage                                      | Ad version                              |
| --------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Stop tracking your license in a spreadsheet.  | Spreadsheets aren't a tracker.          |
| Stay ahead of your IEMA renewal.              | IEMA renews. Be ready.                  |
| Every CE credit, every renewal, one screen.   | One screen. Every credit.               |

## What success looks like on paid

- **CTR > 1%** on LinkedIn feed (industry baseline 0.4%).
- **Cost per signup**: track it. Don't shrink the headline to optimize for it.
- **Specific subject-line testing**: test the number vs. the question vs. the imperative — not "with emoji vs. without" or any other formatting gimmick.
- **What's not success**: a viral post. We don't optimize for virality; we optimize for the right tech finding us.
