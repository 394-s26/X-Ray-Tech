# Vocabulary

The specific word list X-Ray Tech uses — and the words it doesn't.

## Words we prefer

### About us

- *X-Ray Tech* (the product name, full hyphen, capital X and capital R+T)
- *the team behind X-Ray Tech*
- *what we built*

Never: *our platform, our solution, our ecosystem, the system.*

### About the user

- *technologist* (full word, not "tech" in long-form copy)
- *manager / lead tech*
- *you* and *your team*

Never: *user, customer, end user, professional, healthcare professional, HCP, individual.*

### About outcomes

- *log a certification*
- *earn credits / earn points*
- *renew your license*
- *stay current*
- *catch up before renewal*
- *audit-ready*

Never: *grow, scale, optimize, transform, accelerate, supercharge, unlock, unleash.*

## Vertical-specific vocabulary

X-Ray Tech serves **one** vertical — radiologic technology — but with two distinct roles inside it.

### Role: individual technologist

- **People**: technologist, tech, RT(R), staff radiographer
- **Operations**: shift, modality (X-ray, CT, MRI, mammography), scan, patient throughput
- **Moments**: renewal cycle, biennial reporting, audit, license lapse, scope change

### Role: imaging manager

- **People**: imaging manager, lead tech, department director, compliance officer, HR liaison
- **Operations**: team roster, coverage, renewal calendar, expiring credentials, audit response
- **Moments**: 30-day-out / 90-day-out warnings, monthly review, annual audit

### Their licensing world (use this language verbatim)

- ARRT (American Registry of Radiologic Technologists)
- IEMA (Illinois Emergency Management Agency — for Illinois techs)
- CE credit / continuing-education credit / **CE point** (preferred unit)
- Category A vs. Category B credits
- Renewal cycle (typically 2 years)
- Modality-specific CE (e.g., 16 of your 24 must be CT-specific if you hold a CT credential)

## Verbs we use

*log, add, upload, track, see, know, renew, catch up, stay ahead, finish, complete, count, earn, mark, review, sign off.*

Notice: all of these are things a technologist actually does at work. None of them are SaaS verbs.

## Verbs we avoid

*leverage, transform, supercharge, unleash, empower, enable, optimize, streamline, accelerate, revolutionize, disrupt, scale, grow, drive, deliver value, surface insights.*

## Numbers we like

The brand is built on small, concrete integers — the kind a real tech actually uses:

- **CE points**: "18 of 24."
- **Days**: "287 days until your IEMA renewal."
- **Counts**: "4 certifications expiring in 90 days across your team."
- **Percentages**: only when the underlying number is small enough to be honest. "75% of your CE points logged" is fine. "75% improvement in compliance" is hype — don't.
- **Durations of time saved**: cite the user. "Michelle's team saves about 2 hours per month not chasing paper records."

Never round up to make a number sound bigger. "97% of techs" is suspicious. "23 of 24 techs in the pilot" is real.

## The headline recipe

A great X-Ray Tech headline follows one of these three shapes:

1. **Number + noun + outcome.** *"18 of 24 CE points, one screen."*
2. **Imperative + their world.** *"Stay ahead of your IEMA renewal."*
3. **Direct challenge to the status quo.** *"Stop tracking your license in a spreadsheet."*

If the headline doesn't fit one of these three shapes, it's probably trying too hard. Rewrite.
