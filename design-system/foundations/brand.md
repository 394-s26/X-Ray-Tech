# Brand

## Mission

Give X-ray technologists a measurable, calm, real-time view of where they stand against the licenses they have to keep alive — and give the managers above them the same view of their team.

## Vision

Every X-ray tech in the country opens one place each month and knows, in five seconds, exactly how many points stand between them and their next renewal. No spreadsheets. No paper folders. No "I think I'm fine."

## Values

1. **Numbers over narratives.** "On track" is not a status. "18 of 24 CE points earned, 287 days until renewal" is a status.
2. **Their world, not ours.** We speak ARRT, IEMA, CE credits, modality. We do not speak "platform," "solution," or "ecosystem."
3. **Calm by default.** A credential platform should feel like a well-lit hallway, not an air-traffic-control tower. Color stays out of the way until something actually needs attention.
4. **One screen tells the truth.** If the dashboard says you're done, you are done. No buried sub-pages, no fine print.
5. **Built for the manager, used by the tech.** Both sides need to trust the same number.

## Positioning statement

For X-ray technologists and the managers who lead them, **X-Ray Tech** is the credential-tracking platform that turns the question *"am I on track?"* into a number you can read in five seconds. Unlike spreadsheets, paper logs, or the generic HR-compliance portals most hospitals use, X-Ray Tech speaks the technologist's actual licensing world (ARRT, IEMA, modality-specific CE) and shows progress as live, measurable percentages — not "submitted" / "approved" checkboxes.

## Target audience

**Primary** — Individual X-ray technologists who hold ARRT certification and a state license (e.g., IEMA in Illinois), and have to earn 24 CE points per cycle.

**Secondary** — X-ray imaging managers and lead technologists who supervise teams of 4–40 techs and are personally accountable when a team member's license lapses.

**Tertiary** — Imaging department directors, HR compliance officers — read-only stakeholders who want monthly snapshots.

## Audience insight

Michelle (manager persona) didn't ask for a "compliance platform." She asked for a way to stop opening four different state-board websites, three PDF reports, and a shared Google Sheet just to answer the question her boss asks every Monday: *"is anyone on the team going to lapse in the next 90 days?"*

The product wins not by adding features, but by **collapsing five tools into one screen**.

## Archetype

**The Quiet Steward.** A brand that holds your records the way a good librarian holds a borrower's history — accurately, without theater, and ready the moment you ask. It does not perform; it knows.

## Anti-archetype

**The Cheerleader.** No confetti when you log a certification. No "🎉 Way to go!" toasts. No streak counters. No gamification. The reward is *the number going up*. That's the only reward.

Also not: the **Compliance Watchdog**. We are not a "system of record" tone — we don't speak in policy clauses or audit language.

## The one-sentence promise

Always know exactly where you stand against your license — to the point.
