# LinkedIn

## Aspect ratios

| Format               | Aspect | Pixels     | Use                                |
| -------------------- | ------ | ---------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Carousel (PDF)**   | 1:1    | 1200×1200  | **Default for thought-leadership** |
| Single image post    | 1.91:1 | 1200×627   | Article share, big-number callout  |
| Banner (company page)| 4:1    | 1584×396   |                                    |

## Carousel structure (5–10 frames)

1. **Cover** — the hook. One number, one direct claim, one question. Lockup bottom-right.
2. **Teaching frames (3–8)** — each frame: one heading + one sentence + optional visual. The audience is managers and operators; respect their time.
3. **Takeaway** — the synthesized insight. One frame.
4. **CTA frame** — one line: "See how X-Ray Tech does this. Free for every tech and manager. xraytech.app."

## Post types

### Text-only thought leadership

For LinkedIn feed posts (no image). Format:

```
[Hook — one line, blunt, no setup.]

[Two short paragraphs (≤3 sentences each) developing the idea.]

[Concrete example, named: a real practice, a real number.]

[One-line takeaway.]

[Optional: link to a case study or article.]
```

No "1/" or "🧵 Thread" framing. Just the post.

### Case study post

Pin the number first, the story second.

```
2 hours.

That's how long Michelle's team used to spend on the first Monday of every month, reconciling CE-credit spreadsheets across 14 technologists.

After they moved to X-Ray Tech, Michelle pulls one screen and answers her director's "who's renewing in 90 days?" question in 30 seconds.

We wrote up the whole story → [link]
```

### Number post (big-stat)

Single 1200×627 image:
- Background `paper`.
- One `mono-xl` number (160–200px on the image, `brand/600`), centered.
- One line below in `h2`.
- Source line in `caption`.
- Lockup bottom-right.

### Hiring post

```
We're hiring a [role].

You'd work directly with [team size] people, including Michelle — the imaging manager who originally asked us to build X-Ray Tech.

Day-to-day: [3 bullet points, specific].

[Compensation range. Or: "We pay above market for this role."]

If you've worked on dashboards for a specific kind of professional before, we want to talk.

[Link to apply]
```

## Company page

- **Banner**: `paper` background, lockup centered-left, one line of `body-lg` ink/700 to its right: "Credential tracking for X-ray technologists." A single grid-pattern wash on the right third.
- **Logo (square)**: `mark.svg` rendered on `paper`, no enclosing background — LinkedIn auto-crops to a circle, which works because the aperture mark is already circular and dead-centered.
- **Tagline**: "Stay ahead of your license."
- **About**: the paragraph boilerplate from `voice/homepage-copy.md`.

## What NOT to do on LinkedIn

- "🚀 Excited to announce…"
- The "shocking truth about X-ray credentialing nobody is talking about" hook.
- AI-generated cover graphics with floating purple orbs.
- Tagging dozens of accounts for engagement.
- Polls about whether AI will replace radiologic technologists. Don't.
- "I asked ChatGPT about [topic] — here's what it said." Banned.

## Post frequency

- 1–2 carousels per month (high-effort, focused).
- 1–2 text-only posts per week (low-effort, founder voice).
- Reshare each case study once with a different hook.
- Never post for the sake of posting. Empty days are on-brand.
