# X-Ray Tech License CE Logic (Illinois)

Illinois x-ray technologists typically maintain **two separate credentials**:

- **ARRT** — national certification/registration ([arrt.org](https://www.arrt.org))
- **IEMA** — Illinois state accreditation, issued via the IEMA Division of Nuclear Safety

Both require **24 CE credits every 2 years**. In practice, techs "double dip" — the same CE certificates can satisfy both — **but the cycle windows, deadlines, and probation rules differ**, so the system must track them independently.

---

## Universal rules

### Certificates are single-use per license

A CE certificate, regardless of its expiration date or how long it remains valid, **can never be applied more than once within the same license**.

- A single certificate can be applied to **one ARRT cycle** AND **one IEMA cycle** (double dipping across licenses is allowed).
- It **cannot** be reused across two ARRT cycles or two IEMA cycles.
- It **cannot** be reused between a probation period and a new cycle within the same license (see ARRT probation below).

### Category requirement

Both ARRT and IEMA require CE certificates to be **Category A or A+**. Certificates outside these categories do not count toward the 24-credit requirement for either license.

---

## ARRT (National)

### Cycle window
- **Starts:** first day of the user's **birth month**
- **Ends:** last day of the month **before** their birth month, 2 years later
- **Length:** 2 years

### CE crediting rule
- CEs earned **during the birth month** (the final month of the cycle) do **NOT** count toward that cycle.
- The effective earning window ends the last day of the month **before** the birth month at cycle close.

### Probation
- Triggered when a user has **< 24 points** at cycle end.
- **Duration:** 6 months.
- Includes a probation fee; unresolved deficiency can lead to discontinuation of the credential.
- CEs earned during probation apply **only to the deficient (previous) cycle** — they **cannot** be applied to the new cycle that has already started.
- The new cycle runs in parallel and begins at **0 points**.

---

## IEMA (Illinois)

### Cycle window
- **Starts:** the month the user was **first accredited**
- **Length:** 2 years
- **Reporting deadline:** last day of the accreditation month at cycle close

### CE crediting rule
- CEs may be earned at **any point** in the cycle, **including the final month**, as long as they are reported by the cycle's last day.

### Probation
- No publicly documented IEMA equivalent to ARRT's formal 6-month CE probation has been found.
- IEMA does retain independent authority to issue, deny, or discipline accreditation, so non-compliance is not consequence-free — it just isn't governed by a defined CE probation window the way ARRT is.

---

## Key differences at a glance

| Rule | ARRT | IEMA |
|---|---|---|
| Cycle anchor | Birth month | Accreditation month |
| Final-month CEs count? | **No** | **Yes** (if reported by deadline) |
| Formal CE probation | 6 months, isolated points pool | None documented |
| Points required | 24 | 24 |
| Accepted CE category | A or A+ | A or A+ |
| Cert reuse within same license | Not allowed | Not allowed |
| Cert reuse across licenses | Allowed (one ARRT + one IEMA) | Allowed (one ARRT + one IEMA) |
